Learning Tekken Has Never Been Easier: Introducing Okizeme.gg

by William Hernandez August 15, 2025


The Tekken series is notoriously hard to get into, thanks to its vast array of complex 3D movements. Every character has so many different moves that they can use, and it takes a long time before players can perfectly visualize what their inputs will do. Fortunately, Tekken 8 players now have an incredible tool at their disposal that will make the learning process significantly easier.

Okizeme.gg is an easy-to-search database for Tekken 8 moves. Not only do you get the frame data, but there's an example video for every move that a character can perform. While players will still need to practice their combos, a visual guide like Okizeme is a godsend to have at your disposal.

Note: As of the time of writing, not every move has an associated video. The plan is to have complete coverage, though.

The Definitive Tekken 8 Database

Every single move in Tekken 8 can be found on Okizeme. Just select a character on the database page, and you'll be able to see their entire move list. Hovering over a specific move will play a short clip that automatically loops. Honestly, Okizeme is better for reviewing moves than the actual game itself. You can look through them a lot faster without needing to do any testing of your own. Again, the frame data is also there for easy viewing. You can also change the notation to your preferred style.

A resource like this is useful for players of all skill levels. Even just knowing what a move is supposed to look like can help with pattern recognition. Novice players especially need that kind of visual reinforcement as they learn their characters. More advanced players can use this database to improve their defense. Knowing how to react properly once you see a certain animation can make a big difference during fights, and you definitely should know if your attacks are leaving you more vulnerable.

Dive Deep Into The Move Properties

Okizeme.gg offers more detailed descriptions to the majority of moves available. Special properties like Force Crouch and Tornado are clearly acknowledged, but the site offers even more information beyond that. For example, Heihachi's Hammer Beating attack includes an extra set of notes outlining all of the move's capabilities. The site will even tell you if you can directly transition into a unique character stance as a follow-up to a move. It's definitely worth giving the site a visit, if you want to learn a character more deeply.
